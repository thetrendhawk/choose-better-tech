# Choose Better Tech
## Autonomous Batch Publishing Charter — Data Removal Cluster, Batch 01

**Status:** Approved  
**Owner:** Aaron Evans  
**Execution partner:** ChatGPT  
**Primary objective:** Publish a commercially useful, trustworthy data-removal content cluster that can attract search traffic, help readers make decisions, and support future affiliate income.

## 1. Purpose

This charter authorizes ChatGPT to independently research, write, implement, publish, test, and verify a defined batch of Choose Better Tech articles after Aaron approves the direction.

Aaron retains control of the overall topic cluster, commercial direction, major editorial principles, prohibited approaches, and changes that materially alter the site or business strategy. ChatGPT handles normal execution without seeking article-by-article approval.

## 2. Business objective

Choose Better Tech is intended to become a sustainable income-producing content business.

This batch should therefore pursue four goals at the same time:

1. Help readers make better decisions.
2. Build topical authority around data-removal services.
3. Target search queries with meaningful traffic or commercial intent.
4. Create honest paths to affiliate revenue where appropriate.

An article should not be published merely because an affiliate program exists. When two topics offer comparable reader value and evidence quality, priority may be given to the topic with stronger realistic monetization potential.

## 3. Reader promise

Every article must leave the reader better equipped to make a decision. The content must explain who a product is for, who should not buy it, distinguish documented facts from marketing claims, disclose meaningful limitations, discuss recurring cost and renewal implications, avoid unsupported universal “best” claims, and make a clear recommendation based on reader fit.

The goal is not to push the reader toward a purchase. The goal is to help the right reader purchase confidently and help the wrong reader avoid wasting money.

## 4. Approved batch direction

**Cluster:** Data-removal services  
**Working batch size:** Eight articles  
**Primary audience:** Privacy-conscious beginners and ordinary consumers who want to reduce exposure on people-search sites and data-broker databases  
**Geographic emphasis:** United States, unless an article clearly states otherwise  
**Commercial emphasis:** Reviews and comparisons supported by practical educational content

### Initial article slate

1. Optery Review
2. Incogni Review
3. DeleteMe Review
4. Optery vs Incogni
5. Incogni vs DeleteMe
6. Optery vs DeleteMe
7. How Long Does Data Removal Take?
8. Can Your Information Reappear After Data Removal?

ChatGPT may refine titles, URLs, article order, and exact framing based on current search intent, evidence availability, internal-linking needs, and commercial value. ChatGPT may replace an article only when the replacement better serves the batch goal and remains inside the approved data-removal cluster.

## 5. Publishing order

The default order is individual product reviews, product comparisons, supporting educational articles, then hub updates and internal-link improvements. The first article will be the Optery review unless research reveals a material reason not to publish it.

Articles may be released sequentially so each deployment can be checked before the next article is added.

## 6. Monetization standard

Monetization is a core business objective for every article, but it must be implemented responsibly.

For each article, ChatGPT should evaluate affiliate-program availability, current approval status, permitted marketing practices, geographic restrictions, commission structure when known, cookie duration when known, allowed landing pages, trademark or paid-search restrictions, disclosure requirements, and whether the affiliate relationship creates an editorial conflict.

### Affiliate-link rules

- Affiliate links may be used only for approved programs.
- Links must include appropriate disclosure and sponsored attributes.
- Affiliate availability must never determine the factual conclusion.
- A non-affiliate product may outrank an affiliate product.
- Existing ordinary links must not be silently converted into affiliate links without confirming the program and destination.
- Promotional pricing must be treated as temporary unless proven otherwise.
- Savings claims must be based on verifiable current terms.
- No fabricated scarcity, countdowns, ratings, discounts, or “exclusive” offers.
- No claim that Choose Better Tech personally tested a service unless real testing occurred.

### Conversion goal

Each commercially oriented article should offer a natural next step for the right reader, such as visiting the provider, starting a free scan, reviewing current plans, reading a comparison, or using a manual opt-out guide instead of purchasing.

Calls to action should match the article’s conclusion rather than forcing every reader toward a paid product.

## 7. Evidence standard

Research should prioritize official product documentation, privacy policies and terms, pricing and plan pages, security or technical documentation, audit reports or independent assessments, reputable independent testing and reporting, and credible user experience evidence used cautiously.

Vendor statements must be described as vendor statements unless independently verified.

The article must clearly identify uncertainty involving broker counts, removal success rates, processing times, reappearance rates, pricing, plan coverage, geographic availability, recurring monitoring, and custom-removal claims. A large advertised broker count must not automatically be presented as better coverage.

## 8. Hands-on testing rule

ChatGPT must never imply that Aaron or Choose Better Tech personally tested a product unless actual testing occurred.

Without hands-on testing, approved language includes “According to the company’s documentation,” “The service says,” “The sources reviewed indicate,” “We could not independently verify,” and “Public documentation does not make clear.”

Prohibited language includes “In our testing,” “We found the app easy to use,” “Our scan discovered,” and “We successfully removed,” unless those actions genuinely happened and evidence was recorded.

## 9. Article requirements

Every product review should normally include a clear verdict, best fit, poor fit, how the service works, free features or scan options, paid-plan differences, coverage and broker-count limitations, proof or reporting features, privacy implications, expected timing, reappearance and monitoring, cancellation or renewal considerations, alternatives, final recommendation, and source-aware limitations.

Every comparison should normally include a concise outcome by reader type, side-by-side feature differences, pricing uncertainty, workflow differences, coverage limitations, privacy considerations, best choice by use case, and situations where neither service is necessary.

## 10. SEO and commercial-intent rules

Each article should target a distinct primary search intent and avoid unnecessary cannibalization. ChatGPT may optimize title, heading structure, URL slug, description, internal anchor text, related-article placement, schema, introductory framing, and FAQ coverage.

SEO must not produce keyword stuffing, repetitive headings, false freshness, unsupported “best” claims, copied competitor structures, or filler written only to increase length. Commercial-intent articles should answer buying questions early rather than hiding the conclusion.

## 11. Internal-linking standard

Each new article must strengthen the existing cluster. Relevant links may include `/data-removal`, `/best-data-removal-services`, `/are-data-removal-services-worth-it`, related product reviews, related comparisons, and privacy-policy or editorial-standard pages where appropriate.

Existing articles may be updated to link to new content when the link improves reader navigation. Internal links must be contextually useful, not inserted merely to raise link counts.

## 12. Editorial metadata

Every new article must include visible author or reviewer identity, accurate updated date, publication date when supported, Article or appropriate structured data, canonical path, title and meta description, and disclosure where commercial links appear.

The reusable metadata system should be used rather than duplicating one-off implementations.

## 13. Autonomous authority

After approval, ChatGPT is authorized to determine the final publishing sequence, refine titles and search intent, conduct current research, write all articles in the batch, create required data and page files, update routes and route manifests, update sitemap outputs, add metadata and structured data, add internal links, update category pages and content indexes, create appropriate affiliate link placements for already approved programs, run available checks, push completed work to `main`, monitor Vercel deployment, investigate and fix ordinary build errors, verify live URLs, make minor layout or copy corrections, maintain a publishing log, and report completed work to Aaron.

Routine implementation choices do not require separate approval.

## 14. Required stop conditions

ChatGPT must pause and ask Aaron before proceeding when a new topic category outside data removal is needed, a new affiliate program must be joined or accepted, affiliate terms appear legally or commercially risky, a conclusion conflicts with an existing site recommendation, evidence is too weak for a responsible article, a product appears deceptive or unsafe to promote, a material privacy or legal concern emerges, a major site redesign is required, important existing content would need to be deleted, a live URL or established taxonomy would need to change, a claim would require hands-on testing that has not happened, GitHub/Vercel/analytics/routing/deployment behavior becomes abnormal, or production changes cannot be confidently verified.

ChatGPT should not stop for ordinary wording, formatting, linking, routing, metadata, or minor implementation decisions.

## 15. Quality gate before publication

Before every article goes live, ChatGPT must verify that the search intent is distinct, major factual claims are supported, vendor claims are properly attributed, affiliate disclosures are present where needed, pricing language acknowledges change, no fake hands-on experience is implied, verdict and audience fit are clear, important limitations are visible, internal links work, metadata is correct, the route is recognized, the build succeeds, the live page returns correctly, and the article appears in the appropriate hub or index.

An article that fails this gate should not be published merely to complete the batch.

## 16. Reporting

After each successful publication, ChatGPT should report the article title, live URL, commit, deployment status, main verdict, monetization status, affiliate link status, unresolved evidence limitations, internal links added, and next article in the batch.

At the end of the batch, ChatGPT should provide a cluster report covering all published URLs, content coverage achieved, affiliate opportunities activated, affiliate opportunities still unavailable, internal-link structure, remaining content gaps, recommended next batch, and early performance signals when analytics data exists.

## 17. Success criteria

This batch is successful when all approved articles are published or responsibly replaced, the data-removal hub becomes a complete decision path, readers can move from education to review to comparison, commercial articles contain honest conversion opportunities, monetization is integrated without compromising recommendations, deployments are stable, and future batches can use the same system with less supervision.

## 18. Governing principle

**Choose Better Tech should earn income because it helps readers make good decisions—not because it pushes the highest-paying product.**

Trust is the mechanism that makes long-term monetization possible.
