# Editorial Objective

Create an evidence-based Optery review for mainstream privacy buyers.

The article must help readers understand whether Optery is a good fit for their needs without exaggeration, unsupported rankings, hype, or affiliate influence.

The review should position Optery as strong for people-search exposure visibility, screenshot-based reporting, beginner-friendly exposure discovery, supported-country users who want documented removal workflows, families, and public-facing professionals who want proof. It should clearly explain why Optery is weaker for users expecting full internet deletion, permanent removals, public-record removal, social-media cleanup, dark-web remediation, broad search-engine deletion, or users who prefer disciplined manual opt-out control.

# Target Reader

Primary reader:

- Beginner privacy buyer
- Mainstream consumer worried about people-search exposure
- User seeing personal information on broker or people-search sites
- Public-facing professional
- Household or family privacy buyer
- User comparing Optery against Incogni, DeleteMe, Privacy Bee, Kanary, or manual opt-out

# Editorial Guardrails

Do not say Optery deletes users from the internet.

Do not imply removals are permanent.

Do not imply Optery removes everything from Google.

Do not imply Optery removes court records, government records, public records, social media posts, blogs, forums, dark-web leaks, or all copied / republished data.

Do not imply screenshot reports prove every broker record is gone.

Do not overstate Optery's effectiveness.

Do not ignore Consumer Reports or academic evidence showing category-wide limits.

Do not ignore that manual opt-out can outperform paid services in narrow tests.

Do not ignore the privacy tradeoff of giving Optery identifying data.

Do not treat SOC 2 or security claims as a public audit of every outcome.

Do not hard-code pricing unless clearly marked as volatile and sourced from the approved research.

Do not hard-code affiliate links.

Do not let affiliate availability or commercial status influence editorial conclusions.

Do not invent testing results.

Do not create unsupported rankings.

Preserve uncertainty where the research identifies unresolved questions.

# Required Nuance

The review should clearly explain:

- Optery is strongest for people-search exposure visibility, screenshot reporting, and users who want proof.
- Data removal is ongoing maintenance, not a one-time permanent deletion.
- Data can reappear.
- Screenshot proof is strong for public people-search removals but is not universal proof across every broker.
- Optery does not remove public records, government records, social media posts, dark-web exposure, or all Google results.
- Consumer Reports and academic evidence show that the category remains limited.
- Optery may be better than Incogni for users who want screenshot-based proof and transparency.
- Incogni may be better for users who want simpler automation and broader geographic reach.
- Manual opt-out may be better for disciplined DIY users who want full control.
- Optery requires enough identifying data to match and request removals on the user's behalf.
- Optery's privacy and security posture appears above average for the category, but not risk-free.
- Optional OpenAI report analysis and cookie/ad-tech privacy caveats should be explained plainly.
