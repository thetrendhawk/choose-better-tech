# Editorial Objective

Create an evidence-based comparison between 1Password and Proton Pass for mainstream password manager buyers.

The comparison must help readers decide which product better fits their needs without declaring a universal winner, inventing unsupported claims, browsing for new facts, or allowing affiliate/business status to influence conclusions.

# Search Intent

Primary intent:

Commercial investigation.

The reader is likely comparing 1Password and Proton Pass before deciding which password manager better fits their priorities around premium polish, family workflows, recovery, Travel Mode, privacy transparency, free-plan value, aliases, and ecosystem fit.

# Core Thesis

1Password is generally the better reader-fit choice for users who want premium polish, mature family workflows, strong recovery and sharing experience, Travel Mode, and a long-established password-manager experience.

Proton Pass is generally the better reader-fit choice for privacy-conscious users, open-source transparency, strong free-plan value, alias/email privacy, and Proton ecosystem users.

Neither product should be framed as universally better. Bitwarden should be mentioned as the stronger alternative for self-hosting and open-source maximalists. NordPass should be mentioned as the simpler/value-oriented alternative, especially for Nord ecosystem users.

# Editorial Guardrails

Do not declare a universal winner.

Do not claim either product is perfect.

Do not claim 1Password is open source.

Do not claim Proton Pass is self-hostable.

Do not claim Proton Pass is more mature than 1Password for premium polish, family workflow depth, Travel Mode, or enterprise maturity.

Do not claim 1Password is better than Proton Pass for free-plan seekers, open-source transparency, aliases, or Proton ecosystem users.

Do not claim either product is better than Bitwarden for self-hosting/open-source maximalists.

Do not overstate 1Password's Secret Key as making it categorically safer for every user.

Do not overstate Proton's Swiss jurisdiction or privacy claims.

Do not hard-code pricing unless clearly marked as volatile.

Do not hard-code affiliate links.

Do not let affiliate status influence the comparison verdict.

Preserve uncertainty where either repository marks information as volatile or unresolved.
