# Editorial Objective

Create an evidence-based Incogni review for mainstream privacy buyers.

The article must help readers understand whether Incogni is a good fit for their needs without exaggeration, unsupported rankings, hype, or affiliate influence.

The review should position Incogni as strong for beginners who want hands-off broker removal, mainstream privacy buyers, users who want recurring removal requests, people who want both public and private broker coverage, families up to five members, and users who value process assurance and vendor accountability. It should clearly explain why Incogni is weaker for people expecting full internet deletion, crisis-level stalking or doxxing needs, maximum screenshot-level proof, DIY control, and users whose main issue is Google results, court records, government records, social posts, blogs, forums, or dark-web leaks.

# Editorial Guardrails

Do not say Incogni deletes users from the internet.

Do not imply removals are permanent.

Do not imply Incogni removes everything from Google.

Do not imply Incogni removes court records, government records, public records, social media posts, blogs, forums, dark-web leaks, or all copied / republished data.

Do not sell data removal as absolute identity-theft prevention.

Do not treat Deloitte assurance as an outcome guarantee.

Do not treat Deloitte assurance as a full security audit, encryption test, or legal-compliance warranty.

Do not ignore the privacy tradeoff of giving Incogni identifying data.

Do not ignore people-search count inconsistency.

Do not overstate dashboard statuses as proof that every data field is gone.

Do not overstate Protect plan benefits.

Do not hard-code pricing unless clearly marked as volatile and sourced from the approved research.

Do not hard-code affiliate links.

Do not let affiliate availability or commercial status influence editorial conclusions.

Do not invent testing results.

Do not create unsupported rankings.

Preserve uncertainty where the research identifies unresolved questions.

# Required Nuance

The review should clearly explain:

- Incogni is strongest as a beginner-friendly, automation-first broker-removal service.
- Data removal is ongoing maintenance, not a one-time permanent deletion.
- Data can reappear.
- Incogni covers public and private broker categories, but exact people-search count is inconsistent in Incogni-controlled materials.
- Deloitte assurance supports process claims: broker coverage, recurring request cadence, removal confirmations, request volume, and non-sale of customer data during 2024.
- Deloitte assurance does not prove every user is removed everywhere or that removal is permanent.
- Incogni may not know exactly which fields were removed from broker databases.
- Incogni cannot work without collecting enough identifying data to act on the user's behalf.
- Incogni may be a strong mainstream default for people who want low-friction broker removal.
- Optery may be stronger for screenshot-based proof and public-listing transparency.
- DeleteMe may appeal to users wanting an older U.S.-focused service.
- Privacy Bee may appeal to power users who want broader feature depth and higher-touch privacy extras.
- Kanary may be stronger for high-risk doxxing, harassment, creator, activist, or executive-adjacent threat models.
- Manual opt-out may be better for users who want full control over a narrow set of public listings.
