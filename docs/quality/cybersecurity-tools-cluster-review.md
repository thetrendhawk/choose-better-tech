# Cybersecurity Tools Cluster and Monetization Review

- Review date: 2026-07-13
- Reviewer pass: separate governance/evidence challenge pass
- Scope: cluster approval, shared dossier, and monetization opportunity map

## Mission and intent challenge

The cluster is a natural extension of CBT's existing security coverage and answers distinct device-protection decisions. The initial two-article scope is narrow enough to maintain and does not rely on a single opportunistic affiliate review.

## Evidence challenge

The dossier uses current AV-TEST, annual and test-specific AV-Comparatives, SE Labs, Virus Bulletin, Microsoft, FTC, Commerce, and vendor terms. It distinguishes test windows and does not convert one score into a permanent universal ranking. It correctly preserves testing limitations.

## Candidate challenge

Microsoft Defender, Bitdefender, Norton, ESET, F-Secure, McAfee, Avast/AVG, Avira, Malwarebytes, TotalAV, Trend Micro, Sophos, Kaspersky, and secondary candidates are dispositioned. Kaspersky's U.S. exclusion is grounded in government action rather than distorted lab evidence. TotalAV is included despite conflict risk, and non-affiliate Microsoft Defender remains prominent.

## Monetization challenge

Public program availability is separated from CBT status. Exact unknown commissions, cookies, and network terms remain unverified. The map does not authorize applications or implementation. TotalAV's affiliate-heavy market presence is explicitly treated as a reason for more scrutiny, not preference.

## Corrections required and completed

1. Added the Avast FTC order so strong free-product lab results do not erase material privacy history.
2. Added the U.S. Kaspersky prohibition and separated legal suitability from protection performance.
3. Prevented untested “easy,” “light,” “quiet,” and support-quality labels.
4. Limited the approval to two articles and required independent article gates.

## Decision

**APPROVE FOR MERGE**

The Cybersecurity Tools cluster is approved for Best Antivirus Software followed by TotalAV Review. Research and monetization are sufficiently separated. No article drafting is approved without its dedicated research record and claim ledger.
