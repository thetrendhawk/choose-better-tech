## First-Pass Decision

`RETURN FOR CORRECTION`

The first pass rechecked all 33 Critical/High claims and found three material blockers:

1. **UK ADP scope:** The record said “new UK users,” while Apple's current notice covers UK users who had not already enabled ADP. The notice also described a future disablement period for previously enabled users, but the current transition outcome was not established.
2. **Backup traceability:** IG-22 relied only on CBT synthesis for a Critical safety recommendation. It needed direct provider evidence about synchronized changes/deletions, authoritative backup guidance, and explicit disclosure that CBT had not tested ransomware recovery.
3. **Apple monetization certainty:** The supplement classified iCloud+ as `Unavailable`, while the evidence established only that no public iCloud+ commission path was verified.

## Corrections Completed

- Updated IG-13 and every related research section to use the exact UK population and preserve uncertainty for previously enabled accounts.
- Rewrote IG-22 as an explicit, untested CBT safety recommendation supported by Apple and Google synchronization documentation plus CISA guidance to maintain offline encrypted backups and test restoration.
- Changed the iCloud+ commercial status and recommended action to `Status unverified`; no tracked-link authorization was added.
