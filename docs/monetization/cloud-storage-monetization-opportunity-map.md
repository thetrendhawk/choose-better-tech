## Display-ad assessment

Google AdSense has no published minimum traffic threshold, but requires original, policy-compliant content, site ownership/control, and an adult account holder. That makes it technically accessible before premium networks, not automatically desirable now.

Cloud Storage pages have commercial intent and may attract useful CPMs, but early display ads could weaken CBT's trust positioning and add consent, privacy, performance, and layout burdens. The preferred sequence is:

1. grow the cluster and measure organic sessions, engaged time, and device mix;
2. define ad-density, Core Web Vitals, consent, and blocked-category standards;
3. test ads on lower-risk educational pages rather than verdict boxes or safety-critical guidance;
4. compare incremental revenue with affiliate, email, and lead-magnet performance;
5. remove ads if they materially degrade speed, readability, or reader trust.

Evidence: [AdSense eligibility](https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/9724); [How AdSense works](https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/6242051).

**Recommended action: Apply after traffic growth.**

## Sponsorship and newsletter policy gate

Before accepting any cloud-storage sponsorship, CBT should adopt a written sponsor addendum that states:

- sponsorship buys labeled inventory, not ranking, inclusion, wording, links, or review access;
- the sponsor cannot approve, delay, or suppress independent editorial content;
- sponsor-provided claims require ordinary evidence review;
- sponsored newsletters are labeled at the subject/body level where required;
- subscriber addresses are not transferred or sold;
- performance reports use aggregated campaign metrics;
- sponsor conflicts are disclosed on relevant pages;
- CBT can reject or terminate campaigns that conflict with reader safety, accuracy, or privacy.

## Lead-generation gate

Lead generation is acceptable only when the reader deliberately requests contact or a quote from a named provider. A generic newsletter signup, calculator use, or guide download is not consent to sell or transfer a lead.

Required controls:

- name the recipient before submission;
- explain what data will be transferred and why;
- use an unchecked affirmative-consent control;
- retain proof of consent and define deletion periods;
- prohibit providers from using leads outside the stated request;
- do not change rankings or CTA prominence based on lead value;
- offer a non-commercial path to the same editorial guidance.
