## Distinct Search Intent

Primary intent: readers want to choose a password manager for a household that needs private vaults, shared vaults, recovery, emergency access, offboarding, child or teen access, mixed devices, and reasonable long-term pricing.

This is distinct from:

- `/best-password-managers-for-beginners`, which is a general beginner buying guide.
- `/best-free-password-managers`, which focuses on free-plan limits and no-cost options.
- `/are-password-managers-safe`, which explains vault safety, breaches, zero knowledge, master passwords, MFA, recovery, passkeys, and threat models.
- `/password-manager-vs-browser-passwords`, which answers whether a browser/OS manager is enough versus a dedicated manager.
- Product reviews and pairwise comparisons, which evaluate individual products or two-product decisions.

Repository search found family-fit mentions in the hub and product guides, but no existing `/best-password-managers-for-families` route or article matching the family-plan ranking intent.

## Defensible Recommendation Set

Recommended included providers:

1. 1Password Families — best overall family password manager for families that value polished shared vaults, organizer recovery, and recovery planning.
2. Bitwarden Families — best value family plan and best low-cost structured sharing for families comfortable with a more utilitarian interface.
3. Proton Pass Family — best privacy-focused family option for Proton ecosystem users and families that value aliases and end-to-end encrypted metadata.
4. NordPass Family — best simple mainstream setup for families that want six premium vaults and low-friction onboarding.
5. RoboForm Family — best low-cost/simple household option for families that value form filling and can live with fewer family-plan controls.

Contextual alternatives:

- Apple Passwords — best built-in option for Apple-only families with simple sharing needs.
- Dashlane Friends & Family — strongest member count among evaluated alternatives, but weaker emergency planning and no current CBT review.
- Keeper Family — credible non-affiliate alternative with emergency access and shared folders, but not currently in CBT review coverage.
- Enpass Family — local/cloud-vault alternative for technical families, but vault sharing requires separate vault/cloud credential handling and is less straightforward.
- LastPass Families — not recommended as a primary pick because breach history requires unusually careful evaluation; it remains relevant context because it has a family plan and emergency access.

## Core Editorial Framework

1. Families benefit from structured shared vaults more than from text-message, email, notes, or spreadsheet sharing.
2. Private vaults should remain private unless a person deliberately shares items.
3. A family plan does not mean every account should be shared.
4. Recovery and offboarding matter as much as everyday sharing.
5. Emergency access is not the same as family organizer recovery, trusted contacts, or inheritance planning.
6. Browser-family sharing may be enough for simple single-ecosystem households.
7. Dedicated family plans are usually better for mixed devices, permissions, recovery planning, and portability.
8. Children and teenagers need age-appropriate access, privacy boundaries, and recovery help; a password manager is not a parental-control tool.
9. Long-term value should be judged on renewal pricing and family limits, not just promotional pricing.
10. There is no universal best family password manager.

## Provider Findings

### 1Password Families

Evidence:

- Official pricing lists 1Password Families at $4.49/month promotional annual pricing and $5.99/month regular annual pricing, with a 14-day free trial, up to 5 family members, unlimited shared vaults, and simple admin controls.
- 1Password support says Families includes up to 5 people, each with an account password, and family organizers can manage the account and shared vault access.
- Family organizers can invite members, add guests, create/share vaults, recover family-member accounts, manage billing, and delete the family account.
- 1Password recovery documentation says family organizers can recover another member's account, after which the member receives a new Secret Key and account password; the member can access prior data after recovery completes.
- 1Password recovery-plan guidance says users cannot recover their own account and should designate at least one additional family organizer; 1Password states it cannot reset or recover account passwords or Secret Keys.
- Existing CBT research supports 1Password's Secret Key, Emergency Kit, Watchtower, Travel Mode, family sharing, recovery, passkeys, and audit/transparency posture.

Editorial label:

- Best overall family password manager.
- Best recovery-planning option among included providers.

Limitations:

- No permanent free personal tier found.
- Closed-source main apps.
- Family organizer recovery is powerful but not a substitute for each member saving recovery material.
- Additional family-member pricing beyond the base plan needs rechecking before quoting.

### Bitwarden Families

Evidence:

- Official pricing lists Families at $3.99/month, billed annually at $47.88, for up to 6 users.
- Bitwarden Families includes 6 premium accounts, sharing without an item-count cap in checked plan language, collections without an item-count cap in checked plan language, and organization storage.
- Bitwarden's family page says Families supports 6 Premium accounts, up to 6 people for password sharing, unlimited collections, 5 GB personal storage plus 5 GB organization storage, and a 14-day free trial.
- Bitwarden says each family member has their own private vault and shared passwords live in separate shared collections; members see only what is explicitly shared.
- Bitwarden says canceled Families accounts keep personal vault data; shared collections become inactive but can be recovered if the subscription resumes.
- Bitwarden collection permissions include view, hidden-password, edit, hidden-field, and manage-collection levels; hidden passwords limit but do not prevent access and should be treated as shared credentials.
- Bitwarden emergency access supports view or takeover access, is available to premium users and paid organization members including Families, and uses trusted contacts with a wait-time flow.
- Bitwarden privacy policy says the service is not directed to individuals under 13 or the equivalent local minimum age.
- Existing CBT research supports Bitwarden's open-source/client transparency, public audit posture, self-hosting path, strong free plan, passkeys, and utilitarian UX caveats.

Editorial label:

- Best value family plan.
- Best low-cost structured sharing.
- Best for transparent/open-source-minded families.

Limitations:

- Interface is less polished than 1Password.
- Emergency access is account-level, not a granular inheritance plan for specific collections.
- Hidden-password controls should not be represented as true secrecy from someone with shared credential access.

### Proton Pass Family

Evidence:

- Proton says Pass Family is a 6-user plan.
- Proton Pass Family docs say the plan stores logins, passwords, notes, and payment information with end-to-end encryption; Family includes Pass Plus features such as unlimited aliases, integrated 2FA authenticator, dark web monitoring, and more.
- Proton Pass Family docs say members have private vaults and can create shared vaults with other Family members or other Proton Pass users.
- Proton shared-vault docs say all information in a shared vault is visible to invited members.
- Proton share docs say vault access can be view, edit, or admin, and access can be removed from shared vaults.
- Proton secure-sharing docs say users can share a single item or full vault, generate secure links for non-Proton users, choose expiration and view limits, and revoke access.
- Proton Pass pricing page supports broad device and browser coverage and free/paid privacy protection for core use.
- Proton terms say services are for users at least 13 years old, and minors need parental or legal guardian consent.
- Proton emergency access exists in Proton Pass paid plans in current CBT research, but Pass Family documentation does not appear as strong as 1Password's organizer-recovery docs or Bitwarden's emergency-access docs for family recovery.

Editorial label:

- Best privacy-focused family option.

Limitations:

- Family recovery and emergency-access details are less central/clear than 1Password or Bitwarden in the official docs checked.
- Proton Pass is newer than 1Password and Bitwarden as a dedicated password manager.
- Proton ecosystem value can be excellent, but the article should not overstate it for families that only need password sharing.

### NordPass Family

Evidence:

- NordPass plan docs say the Family plan creates six separate encrypted vaults with Premium features; one vault is for the plan owner, who can share remaining vaults with friends or family.
- NordPass family support says the Family plan enables six separate encrypted vaults for the owner, family, and friends under one subscription.
- NordPass Emergency Access requires Premium or Family to grant access; the recipient must have a NordPass account.
- NordPass Emergency Access lets the grantor accept or decline within 7 days, after which access is granted automatically if no action is taken.
- NordPass warns emergency recipients can see all passwords and notes once access is granted, and no active subscription is required during the emergency-access period.
- NordPass access-level docs note passkeys and secure notes do not have a "Can autofill" permission level, so the lowest level for these item types is "Can view."
- Existing CBT research supports NordPass as a simple mainstream value option with family value, passkeys, XChaCha20 positioning, and closed-source/transparency caveats.

Editorial label:

- Best simple household setup.

Limitations:

- Family plan is more "six Premium vaults" than a deeply documented family-admin/recovery system.
- Emergency access is useful but broad; it grants access to all passwords and notes after approval/wait period.
- Closed source and less transparent than Bitwarden or Proton Pass.

### RoboForm Family

Evidence:

- RoboForm pricing lists Family as up to 5 Premium accounts, $3.98/month billed annually, first-year promotional pricing placeholders in the page copy, renewal at $47.75/year, and a 30-day refund period.
- RoboForm Family page says the plan includes up to 5 Premium accounts, secure sharing, all-device use, private/incognito support, monitoring for weak/duplicate/compromised passwords, emergency access, a built-in TOTP authenticator, Windows app login, and form filling.
- RoboForm FAQ says family members do not see each other's passwords because each member has a separate account.
- RoboForm FAQ says a member can securely share an individual password or create a shared folder for the family.
- RoboForm Emergency Access lets users select trusted contacts to obtain access in death, incapacitation, or recovery situations; more than one contact can be selected, wait periods can vary, and access can be revoked.
- Existing CBT research supports RoboForm's form-filling strength and affordability, with transparency caveats.

Editorial label:

- Best low-cost simple family option.
- Best for form-heavy households.

Limitations:

- Five-account limit is lower than Bitwarden, Proton Pass, NordPass, Dashlane, Enpass, and LastPass.
- Less public security/transparency evidence than Bitwarden, Proton Pass, and 1Password.
- Family controls appear less deep than 1Password/Bitwarden.

### Apple Passwords Shared Groups

Evidence:

- Apple Passwords supports Shared Groups to share passwords, passkeys, and Sign in with Apple credentials with family and trusted contacts.
- Apple's personal-safety guide says shared password groups have Group Owner and Group Member roles; the owner can add/remove members.
- Apple Passwords may be enough for Apple-only households with simple sharing needs.

Editorial status:

- Contextual built-in ecosystem option, not a ranked dedicated password manager.

Limitations:

- Best inside Apple devices and Apple account workflows.
- Weaker for mixed-device households and dedicated family-plan recovery/offboarding.

### Dashlane Friends & Family

Evidence:

- Dashlane Friends & Family supports the buyer plus nine members, for 10 accounts total.
- Dashlane says each person's account is independent and data remains private; members can share logins and personal information by choice.
- Dashlane Friends & Family includes unlimited passwords, unlimited devices, import/autofill, collections, secure sharing, and an emergency-data process.
- Dashlane says the plan manager gets VPN, while invited members get Premium features without VPN.
- Dashlane says cancellation can put new Dashlane accounts into export-only mode and that a Friends & Family plan has one manager who cannot transfer the manager account to another email.
- Dashlane says its emergency data process requires sharing a DASH file and password separately; the trusted person can import the data and use it after access.

Editorial status:

- Strong larger-group alternative, but not included as a ranked primary pick because CBT does not yet have a Dashlane review and the emergency workflow is less elegant than 1Password/Bitwarden/Keeper-style emergency access.

### Keeper Family

Evidence:

- Keeper Family docs say the plan supports up to 5 family members with private vaults, at least 10 GB secure file storage, and unlimited password storage.
- Keeper sharing docs say Family users can share records or use Shared Folders, with permissions such as view, edit, or share.
- Keeper emergency access lets users designate up to five emergency contacts and choose a delay period up to three months; it is available for consumer accounts.

Editorial status:

- Strong non-affiliate alternative, but not included as a ranked primary pick because CBT does not yet have a Keeper review and the current page can cover enough primary family options with existing review architecture.

### Enpass Family

Evidence:

- Enpass pricing lists Family at $3.99/month for first 12 months, billed yearly, with up to 6 members, unlimited items, unlimited vaults, unlimited devices, passkey support, breach alerts, and email/forum support.
- Enpass says any vault stored in a personal cloud account can be shared with trusted Enpass users, but sharing requires a separate vault, the vault password, and login credentials for the cloud account hosting the vault.

Editorial status:

- Technical/local-vault alternative, not included as a primary family pick because sharing is less straightforward for mainstream households.

### LastPass Families

Evidence:

- LastPass Families supports up to 6 users at $4/month in current official/near-official materials.
- LastPass has emergency access in paid consumer plans.
- LastPass's 2022 incident history requires nuance: LastPass first said there was no evidence of access to encrypted vaults in an August 2022 development-environment disclosure, then later disclosed a more serious 2022 incident involving copied customer vault data in later updates.

Editorial status:

- Excluded from primary recommendations because the breach history and trust damage require careful reader handling. It can be mentioned as a historical/contextual alternative, not a top pick.

## Conflicts and Uncertainty

- 1Password pricing has promotional and regular annual pricing; use both or qualitative language and avoid unsupported savings math.
- Bitwarden marketing copy says hidden-password permissions exist, but its documentation warns hidden passwords should still be treated as shared credentials.
- Proton Pass Family has strong sharing docs, but family recovery and emergency-access details are less visible than 1Password or Bitwarden.
- NordPass Family is documented as six separate Premium vaults; do not overstate shared-vault or admin granularity.
- RoboForm pricing page contains template placeholders for first-year promotional variables; quote renewal and billed-annual terms carefully.
- Dashlane pricing page did not expose exact price in fetched text; avoid quoting a price.
- Keeper pricing page did not expose exact current price in fetched text; avoid quoting a price.
- Enpass sharing requires a cloud account and vault password flow; it is more technical than "ordinary shared vault" language suggests.
- LastPass breach history has multiple official updates; article should not compress it into "LastPass is unsafe" or ignore it.
- Child-account rules are under-documented for most providers. Use terms/minimum-age evidence where available and preserve uncertainty.
