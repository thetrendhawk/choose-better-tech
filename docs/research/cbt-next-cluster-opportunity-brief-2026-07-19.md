## Evaluation criteria

Each candidate was assessed for:

1. beginner usefulness;
2. adjacency to existing CBT authority;
3. search-intent distinctness;
4. first-hand testing feasibility;
5. evidence quality available;
6. volatility and maintenance burden;
7. internal-link opportunity;
8. monetization potential, considered separately from editorial merit;
9. trust, legal, privacy, and claims risk;
10. minimum viable cluster size and operational cost.

Scores use a 1–5 scale where 5 is most favorable. Maintenance burden and risk are reverse-scored, so a higher score means easier or safer to manage.

## Option 1 — Consumer device security / antivirus

### External facts

- AV-TEST continues to publish recurring Windows 11 home-user evaluations using current public product versions and realistic threat scenarios, giving CBT an independent evidence layer beyond vendor claims. In early 2026, Microsoft Defender received full category scores in protection, performance, and usability in one published test cycle. [AV-TEST Microsoft Defender, Jan–Feb 2026](https://www.av-test.org/en/antivirus/home-windows/windows-11/february-2026/microsoft-defender-antivirus-consumer-4.18-261115/)
- AV-TEST evaluated 14 home-user security products during March–April 2026, showing a sufficiently broad comparison set for a consumer decision cluster. [AV-TEST Windows 11 home-user testing](https://www.av-test.org/en/antivirus/home-windows/windows-11/april-2026/)
- A 2026 consumer survey summary reported that free antivirus usage had grown while paid usage declined, and that cost, distrust, and uncertainty remain meaningful adoption barriers. This supports beginner decision intent around whether built-in protection is enough and when paid suites add value. [Security.org antivirus consumer report](https://www.security.org/antivirus/antivirus-consumer-report-annual/)
- Gen Digital, which owns several major consumer-security brands, reported strong demand for consumer cybersecurity products, but commercial demand does not establish editorial merit or product quality. [Reuters, May 6, 2025](https://www.reuters.com/business/avast-antivirus-owner-gen-digital-forecasts-upbeat-2026-strong-demand-2025-05-06/)

### Inferences

- The strongest beginner question is not simply “Which antivirus is best?” It is “Do I need to pay for antivirus when Windows already includes protection?”
- This category fits CBT’s existing privacy and security authority without duplicating VPN or password-manager intent.
- Independent lab data can anchor malware-protection claims, while CBT first-hand testing can focus on setup friction, alerts, upsells, performance, cancellation, family/device coverage, and clarity.
- The category offers natural internal links to VPNs, password managers, data-removal services, and future scam-protection guidance.

### Risks

- Security marketing often uses fear, urgency, and vague “total protection” language.
- Paid suites bundle VPNs, password managers, identity monitoring, cleanup tools, parental controls, and cloud backup, creating overlap with existing CBT categories.
- Malware testing must not be performed casually on a normal personal machine.
- Rankings can become distorted by high affiliate payouts.

### Risk controls

- Do not execute live-malware testing.
- Use independent lab results for malware-detection claims.
- Use clean test devices or virtual machines for installation, performance, prompts, and removal testing.
- Score bundled features separately from core antivirus protection.
- Treat affiliate availability as a commercial note, never as a ranking factor.
- Include a strong “Windows Security may be enough for many users” editorial baseline.

## Option 2 — Cloud-storage expansion

### Repository facts

CBT already has five approved cloud-storage assets covering beginner selection, secure storage, Proton Drive, Google Drive versus Dropbox, and iCloud versus Google Drive.

### Inferences

- Expansion would be operationally efficient because design, internal-link, and research patterns already exist.
- The strongest remaining distinct intents are likely family storage, photo backup, small-business collaboration, storage for students, and privacy-versus-collaboration tradeoffs.
- However, adding more cloud-storage pieces now risks expanding breadth before existing assets establish performance and indexing baselines.

### Reasons not to prioritize now

- The category already has a credible minimum cluster.
- New pieces could cannibalize the beginner and secure-storage pages unless intent boundaries are exceptionally disciplined.
- Current plans, storage limits, AI features, and bundle pricing change frequently.
- The marginal authority gain is likely lower than opening a genuinely new but adjacent cybersecurity category.

## Option 3 — AI productivity tools

### External facts

- A current market-research summary estimates continuing growth in AI productivity software, driven by assistants, workflow orchestration, and integration into business tools. [Grand View Research, June 2026](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/ai-productivity-tools-market-report)
- Recent research and reporting show real adoption growth but mixed evidence about measurable productivity, quality, review burden, governance, and security. This makes the category important but difficult to evaluate responsibly. [Developers in the Age of AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21305) and [Developer Productivity with GenAI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.24265)

### Inferences

- AI productivity has large demand and future relevance.
- It is currently a weaker topical bridge from CBT’s strongest authority clusters.
- Product churn, model changes, pricing changes, feature gating, and subjective workflow outcomes create an unusually high maintenance burden.
- A trustworthy CBT cluster would require defined workflows, repeated first-hand tests, privacy reviews, data-retention analysis, and task-specific outcome measures—not generic feature summaries.

### Reasons not to prioritize now

- The category would require a new testing framework before publication.
- Broad “best AI tools” content is crowded, volatile, and prone to shallow affiliate-driven recommendations.
- CBT should first establish a narrower point of view, such as privacy-conscious AI productivity for beginners, before committing to a full cluster.

## Option 4 — Consumer online backup

### Inferences

- Online backup is adjacent to both cloud storage and cybersecurity but has distinct user intent: recovery after device failure, ransomware, deletion, or disaster.
- It creates useful educational opportunities around sync versus backup, version history, restore testing, encryption, retention, and recovery speed.
- The category could eventually become a strong bridge between cloud storage and device security.

### Reasons not to prioritize first

- Trustworthy testing requires time-consuming upload and restore trials, including large datasets and failure scenarios.
- Some products overlap heavily with cloud storage or security-suite bundles.
- A small backup cluster is attractive, but consumer device security offers a larger and more immediate beginner decision surface.

## Recommendation

### Recommended next cluster: Consumer device security / antivirus

This is the strongest next CBT expansion because it combines:

- high beginner usefulness;
- direct adjacency to VPN and password-manager authority;
- distinct search intent;
- strong independent evidence infrastructure;
- feasible first-hand usability testing without unsafe malware handling;
- substantial internal-link opportunities;
- realistic monetization that can remain separate from editorial scoring.

The category should not begin with a generic affiliate ranking. It should begin with the decision question most likely to protect reader trust:

> Is Windows Security enough, and when does paying for antivirus actually make sense?

## Provisional hub-and-spoke structure

### Hub

1. **Antivirus and Device Security Decision Hub**
   - Defines antivirus, endpoint protection, security suites, browser protection, identity add-ons, VPN bundles, and backup add-ons.
   - Routes readers by device, risk level, household size, and willingness to pay.

### First-wave spokes

2. **Do You Still Need Antivirus on Windows 11?**
   - Built-in protection versus third-party software.
   - Primary trust-building article and recommended first publication candidate.

3. **Best Antivirus for Beginners**
   - Commercial decision guide only after the evidence framework and first-hand testing are complete.

4. **Free Antivirus vs Paid Antivirus**
   - Clarifies what payment actually adds and what it does not.

5. **Microsoft Defender vs Paid Antivirus**
   - Evidence-led comparison with no presumption that paid software wins.

6. **Are Antivirus Programs Safe and Private?**
   - Telemetry, browser extensions, certificates, web scanning, account requirements, data collection, and ownership.

7. **Antivirus, VPN, and Password Manager: What Does Each One Actually Do?**
   - Prevents category confusion and creates strong internal linking to mature CBT clusters.

### Second-wave candidates

8. **Best Antivirus for Families**
9. **Best Antivirus for Mac**
10. **Best Free Antivirus**
11. **Security Suite vs Standalone Antivirus**
12. **How to Switch Antivirus Without Leaving Protection Gaps**

The minimum viable cluster is six assets: the hub plus the first five distinct spokes. The category should not launch as a twelve-article batch.

## Required evidence and first-hand testing

Before any product ranking or review is authorized, define and execute a documented test protocol covering:

- supported operating systems and current product versions;
- installation time and account requirements;
- default settings versus recommended settings;
- alert clarity and false-alarm handling;
- browser-extension behavior;
- scan controls and scheduling;
- idle and active performance observations;
- upsells, cross-sells, renewal presentation, and cancellation path;
- device and household limits;
- bundled VPN, password-manager, backup, and identity features;
- privacy policy, telemetry, ownership, and incident history;
- uninstall quality and restoration of Windows Security;
- current independent-lab results from at least two reputable testing organizations where available.

Do not test with live malware on a normal personal system. Any controlled security testing must use a dedicated safe environment and a separately approved protocol.
