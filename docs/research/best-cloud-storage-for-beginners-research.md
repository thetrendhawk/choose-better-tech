# Best Cloud Storage for Beginners — Research Record

Checked: 2026-07-12

## Intent

This guide helps first-time cloud-storage buyers choose by ecosystem, free storage, sync, sharing, collaboration, privacy, recovery, and price. It is the first Cloud Storage phase asset.

## Candidate conclusions

- Google Drive: best overall for mainstream beginners and collaboration; 15 GB is shared with Gmail and Google Photos.
- Dropbox: best for simple file synchronization and cross-platform workflows; Basic starts at 2 GB and Plus currently documents 2 TB.
- iCloud Drive: best for Apple-only users; strongest when device backup, photos, and Apple apps are central.
- Microsoft OneDrive: best for Windows and Microsoft 365 households.
- Proton Drive: best privacy-focused beginner option; official current pricing materials show 5 GB free and end-to-end encryption.

## Important distinctions

- Sync is not backup by itself; deletions and ransomware can synchronize.
- Storage quotas may be shared with email, photos, or device backups.
- Provider-side encryption is not the same as end-to-end encryption.
- Collaboration depth, recovery windows, file-version history, account recovery, and export matter.
- Exact prices and plan limits are volatile.
