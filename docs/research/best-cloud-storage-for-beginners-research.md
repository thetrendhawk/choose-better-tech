## Research Objective

Determine which consumer cloud-storage services give beginners the clearest fit by ecosystem, free allowance, sync behavior, sharing, collaboration, recovery, privacy architecture, platform support, portability, and long-term plan structure.

The article must help readers choose a service without confusing sync with backup, provider-side encryption with end-to-end encryption, or a low annual equivalent with a guaranteed renewal price.

## Distinct Search Intent

Primary reader question: Which cloud-storage service is the easiest sensible starting point for my devices, files, collaboration needs, privacy preferences, and budget?

This intent is distinct from:

- `/comparisons/google-drive-vs-dropbox`: a direct two-product decision;
- future provider reviews: product-specific evaluation;
- future backup guides: independent backup and disaster-recovery decisions;
- password-manager and VPN coverage: separate security tools and threat models.

No duplicate route or page targeting the same primary intent was found. The existing slug remains appropriate.

## Scope

Evaluate documented support for:

- free and entry-level storage;

- Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, Android, and web access;

## Recommendation Framework

### Google Drive — Best mainstream default

Evidence basis:

- 15 GB is available with a Google Account but shared across Drive, Gmail, and Google Photos.
- Google One adds storage and may support family sharing.
- Docs, Sheets, Slides, comments, and sharing make collaboration the clearest mainstream differentiator.

Tradeoffs:

- the free number is not a Drive-only allowance;
- Google-native document formats complicate migration;
- the standard consumer service should not be described as end-to-end encrypted file storage.

### Dropbox — Best focused cross-platform sync

Evidence basis:

- Dropbox Basic currently starts at 2 GB;
- official documentation covers online-only files, desktop/mobile sync, sharing, Backup, and 30-day history on Basic/Plus/Family;
- the product remains less tied to a single office ecosystem.

Tradeoffs:

- 2 GB fills quickly;
- the main consumer paid tier is a large jump for light users;
- sync and version history still do not replace an independent backup.

### iCloud Drive — Best Apple ecosystem fit

Evidence basis:

- automatic Apple Account integration and close ties to Files, Photos, device backups, and Apple apps;
- web and Windows access exist;
- deleted Drive files are generally available in Recently Deleted for 30 days.

Tradeoffs:

- 5 GB is shared with backups, Photos, Mail, and other iCloud data;
- mixed-device households face more friction;
- Advanced Data Protection changes encryption and recovery tradeoffs and should not be implied by default.

### OneDrive — Best Windows and Microsoft 365 fit

Evidence basis:

- 5 GB free Microsoft cloud storage;
- Microsoft 365 Personal/Family include larger OneDrive allocations and Office applications;
- Files On-Demand, File Explorer, Office collaboration, and entire-OneDrive restore add ecosystem value.

Tradeoffs:

- Microsoft currently lists Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, and web, not a Linux desktop app;
- cancellation reduces storage allowance and can interrupt sync, editing, and Outlook mail when over quota;
- restore features depend on subscription and retention conditions.

### Proton Drive — Best privacy-focused beginner fit

Evidence basis:

- current support documents E2EE for file contents, filenames, and folder names;
- mainstream web, Windows, macOS, Android, and iOS/iPadOS access;
- free and paid version-history rules are documented.

Tradeoffs:

- the free account begins at 2 GB and requires onboarding actions to reach 5 GB;
- no Linux desktop app appears in the current supported-app list;
- collaboration breadth is still narrower than Google or Microsoft ecosystems.

### Sync.com — Best private sharing and recovery alternative

Evidence basis:

- 5 GB free positioning;
- E2EE, link controls, Vault, CloudFiles, and mobile photo uploads;
- Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, and web access.

Tradeoffs:

- no official Linux desktop app is documented;
- recovery, link controls, and history vary by plan;
- office-suite collaboration is not its main strength.

### pCloud — Best Linux and flexible-plan alternative

Evidence basis:

- official Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, and Android apps;
- virtual-drive, backup, Rewind, revisions, and Trash features;
- subscription and lifetime plan structures.

Tradeoffs:

- ordinary storage and the separately sold Crypto folder have different privacy properties;
- lifetime means the account holder's lifetime or 99 years under current terms, not perpetual company operation;
- free-account inactivity and downgrade rules matter.

## Candidate Exclusions

### Box

Box remains credible for business collaboration, governance, and integrations. It is excluded from the beginner main picks because the free Individual plan's 250 MB per-file upload ceiling is a material consumer limitation and the product's strongest differentiators are business-oriented.

### MEGA

MEGA remains a credible alternative for broad platform support, user-controlled encryption, and a substantial free allowance. It is excluded from the main picks because variable transfer quotas, recovery-key responsibility, and product complexity make it harder to recommend as a simple default without hands-on workflow testing.

## Conflicting or Ambiguous Evidence

- Proton's free plan is often summarized as 5 GB, but current support explains that new accounts start lower and unlock the full allowance through onboarding actions.
- pCloud's promotional pages and account-specific onboarding can describe free storage differently; the article avoids a fixed free-number claim.
- Cloud providers use “backup” for different products. A synchronized folder, camera upload, computer-folder backup, and independent versioned backup are not interchangeable.
- “End-to-end encryption” scope varies. File contents, names, folder names, thumbnails, metadata, shared links, and collaboration data may receive different treatment.
- Recovery windows differ by plan and can exclude permanently deleted items, unsupported file types, or actions outside the retention period.
- Annual-equivalent prices may require prepayment and renew automatically. The article avoids promotional price rankings.

## Editorial Decision

Include seven documented fits: Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud Drive, OneDrive, Proton Drive, Sync.com, and pCloud. Present Box and MEGA as credible alternatives with explicit exclusion reasons.

The core verdict:

- Google Drive is the easiest mainstream default for collaboration and an existing Google workflow.
- Dropbox is the clearest focused sync choice.
- iCloud Drive is the strongest Apple fit.
- OneDrive is the strongest Windows/Microsoft 365 fit.
- Proton Drive is the strongest privacy-focused mainstream starting point.
- Sync.com is a private sharing/recovery alternative.
- pCloud is the clearest Linux and flexible-plan alternative.

No provider is universally best. Beginners should choose by ecosystem, recovery, platform support, privacy model, and migration cost—and keep an independent backup of irreplaceable files.
