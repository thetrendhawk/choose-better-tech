# Executive Summary

## Overview

Optery is a consumer-facing data-removal service focused on people-search and data-broker exposure. The approved research supports treating it as one of the stronger options in the category, especially for users who want visible proof through exposure reports and screenshot-based removal reports.

### Evidence

| Classification | Confidence | Last Verified | Summary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Independent Finding | Moderate | 2026-07-05 | Consumer Reports found people-search removal services as a category were largely ineffective, with 35% of tracked profiles removed within four months, while Optery was among the better performers in that study. |
| Independent Finding | Moderate | 2026-07-05 | Manual opt-outs outperformed all tested services in the Consumer Reports test, with about a 70% success rate. |
| Academic Finding | Moderate | 2026-07-05 | A 2025 academic preprint found category-wide coverage and accuracy limits, with Optery showing fast removal time but high between-user variance. |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-05 | Optery provides exposure reports, profile links, recurring scans, and screenshot-based removal reports on higher tiers when public profiles can be captured. |

## Primary Value Proposition

Optery's strongest supported value proposition is transparency-oriented people-search cleanup: it helps users identify where they are exposed, request removals, monitor recurrence, and see evidence of work where screenshot capture is possible.

## Editorial Summary

Optery is best framed as a credible, evidence-forward data-removal service for people-search exposure, not as a full internet deletion product. It is strongest for beginners, people who want proof, public-facing professionals, and U.S.-centric or supported-country users focused on broker listings. It is weaker for users expecting permanent deletion, dark-web remediation, public-record removal, social-media cleanup, or full search-engine control.
