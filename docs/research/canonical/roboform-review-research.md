# Executive Summary

## Overview

RoboForm is a mature password manager from Siber Systems with unusually strong form-filling history, broad platform reach, low pricing, and a long operating record. The approved research supports positioning RoboForm as practical and affordable for mainstream users rather than as the category leader for privacy transparency or premium polish.

### Evidence

| Classification | Confidence | Last Verified | Summary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-04 | RoboForm is made by Siber Systems, a Virginia corporation headquartered in Fairfax, Virginia. |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-04 | RoboForm's product history reaches back to 1999, making it one of the older surviving password-manager brands. |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-04 | RoboForm emphasizes password management plus form filling, identity profiles, checkout filling, sharing, emergency access, passkeys, and business administration. |
| Independent Finding | High | 2026-07-04 | Mainstream reviewers broadly see RoboForm as easy to use, affordable, and especially strong at autofill/form filling. |
| Open Question | High | 2026-07-04 | Public technical documentation cites PBKDF2-SHA256 with 4,000 iterations by default; the approved research does not resolve whether this is current production behavior or stale documentation. |

## Primary Value Proposition

RoboForm's research-backed value proposition is practical, affordable password management for users who frequently fill identity, checkout, shipping, billing, and repetitive forms. Its strongest case is form filling plus mainstream value, not maximum transparency or the most modern premium experience.

## Editorial Summary

The approved research supports positioning RoboForm as strong for form-filling-heavy users, mainstream beginners who want practical affordability, households of up to five users, and small businesses that want straightforward admin and sharing features. It is weaker for open-source advocates, privacy maximalists, self-hosting users, users who want the strongest free plan, users who want 1Password-level premium polish, and users who want Proton-style privacy ecosystem and aliases.

# Product Profile

## Product Overview

RoboForm is a password manager and form filler for personal, family, and business use. It supports saved logins, identity profiles, checkout and form filling, passkeys, TOTP, breach monitoring, emergency access, secure sharing, imports, broad platform coverage, and business administration.

# Ease of Use

## Installation

RoboForm supports automatic capture after login/account creation, manual entry, and imports from browsers, other password managers, and CSV files. The approved research frames setup and import paths as straightforward.

## User Interface

Independent reviewers broadly describe RoboForm as easy to use and affordable. The interface is good enough for practical mainstream use, but rarely described as the most polished or modern in the category.

## Form Filling and Identity Profiles

Form filling is RoboForm's clearest differentiator. The approved research identifies identity-based filling for address, contact, checkout, shipping, billing, payment, medical, sweepstakes, family-member, client, and repetitive form workflows. Desktop is the clearer form-filling strength; mobile is useful but less uniformly differentiated.

## Customer Support

Personal paid plans advertise priority email plus weekday live chat/phone in the approved research. Support reputation and live-support availability remain volatile and should be rechecked.

## Beginner Friendliness

RoboForm is a strong beginner fit when the buyer values affordability, imports, form filling, identities, broad platform support, and practical setup over open-source transparency or category-leading premium polish.

# Strengths

- Strong form filling and identity-profile workflows.
- Practical affordability and low pricing.
- Long operating history.
- Broad platform and browser support.
- Good fit for mainstream beginners.
- Good household fit if five users is enough.
- Good small-business fit for practical admin and sharing features.
- Passkeys, TOTP, breach monitoring, emergency access, and secure sharing in the approved research.

---

# Limitations

- Proprietary and closed source.
- Public security documentation is thinner than category transparency leaders.
- PBKDF2 iteration-count documentation remains an important open question.
- Free plan is limited to one device.
- Family plan is capped at five users.
- Android identity-form filling outside the RoboForm browser is limited.
- No 1Password-style Travel Mode found in the reviewed sources.
- Not a Proton-style privacy ecosystem or alias product.
- Not a Bitwarden-style self-hosting or open-source transparency fit.
