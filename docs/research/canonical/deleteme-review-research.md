# Executive Summary

## Overview

DeleteMe is a legitimate, established, human-assisted personal-data removal service operated by Abine, Inc. The approved research supports positioning it as strong for mainstream U.S. users who want a hands-off service focused on people-search and data-broker exposure reduction, and for organizations or public-facing professionals where reporting, support, custom removals, and higher-touch service matter.

### Evidence

| Classification | Confidence | Last Verified | Summary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-08 | DeleteMe is operated by Abine, Inc.; its official materials say founders Rob Shavell, Eugene Kuznetsov, and Andrew Sudbury created DeleteMe in 2010. |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-08 | BBB materials in the approved report identify Abine as formed in 2009, headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, with DeleteMe and IronVest as alternate names. |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-08 | DeleteMe has a long market history and stronger brand maturity than many newer data-removal competitors. |
| Independent Finding | High | 2026-07-08 | Consumer Reports found DeleteMe was a midlevel performer in a people-search removal test, and manual opt-outs outperformed all tested paid services in that narrow test. |
| Academic / Regulatory Context | Moderate | 2026-07-08 | PETS 2025 and FTC context support category-wide limits around broker coverage, match accuracy, incomplete removals, public records, and recurring reappearance. |

## Primary Value Proposition

DeleteMe's strongest supported value proposition is high-touch exposure reduction: human-assisted data broker and people-search removals, recurring monitoring, quarterly reporting, custom removal support, family/multi-person support where available, and mature business/admin workflows.

## Editorial Summary

The approved research supports framing DeleteMe as credible and useful, not as a full internet deletion service. It is strongest for mainstream U.S. consumers, people worried about people-search exposure, busy users who want help, public-facing professionals, families/multi-person users where supported, and business/admin buyers. It is weaker for users optimizing for the best independently measured removal performance, lowest-cost automation, screenshot-heavy proof, DIY control, or removal of public records, social posts, Google results, dark-web data, and the broader internet.

# Strengths

- Established provider with long operating history.
- Human-assisted workflow with privacy advisors and custom removals.
- Quarterly reports and comparatively mature reporting.
- Published site list with plan markers.
- Strong fit for mainstream U.S. people-search/data-broker exposure reduction.
- Good fit for public-facing professionals who value support and custom removals.
- Family/multi-person support is documented where supported.
- Mature business/admin features compared with consumer-only tools.
- Clearer-than-average product-limits language.

---

# Limitations

- DeleteMe cannot delete someone from the internet.
- DeleteMe cannot fully remove public records, court/government records, social media posts, all Google results, or dark-web data.
- Data can reappear after removal.
- Removal is ongoing maintenance, not a one-time permanent fix.
- Consumer Reports found DeleteMe was a midlevel performer in its people-search removal test.
- Consumer Reports found manual opt-outs outperformed all tested paid services in that narrow test.
- The full 976-site claim should not be treated as equivalent to Standard consumer-plan coverage.
- Standard consumer coverage appears materially narrower than the broad master-site-list headline.
- DeleteMe requires sensitive personal identifiers to work.
- Pricing, plan details, country coverage, custom-removal limits, privacy-policy details, business pricing, and support/cancellation signals are volatile.

---

# Ideal Users

- Mainstream U.S. consumers who want a known, established provider.
- People worried about people-search sites showing home address, phone number, relatives, or prior addresses.
- Busy users who want human-assisted help instead of DIY opt-outs.
- Families or multi-person users where supported.
- Public-facing professionals who want custom removals, reporting, and support.
- Businesses or admin buyers that want employee privacy-risk reduction and management features.

---

# Not Recommended For

- DIY/control-focused users willing to perform manual opt-outs.
- Users who want the lowest-cost automation.
- Users who want screenshot-heavy proof and exposure visibility as the main feature.
- Users who expect full internet deletion.
- Users whose main issue is public records, court records, social posts, blogs/forums, dark-web data, or broad Google removal.
- Users outside supported regions where coverage is unclear.
- Buyers optimizing mainly for best independently measured removal performance.
