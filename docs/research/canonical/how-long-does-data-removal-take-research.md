# How Long Does Data Removal Take? — Research Record

**Status:** Approved for implementation  
**Checked:** 2026-07-19  
**Testing basis:** Documentation and independent research only; no service account, removal, broker, support, or timing test was performed.

## Search intent and original contribution

The page answers a timing question that the current reviews, comparisons, hub, and worth-it guide only answer in passing: how long the process takes from discovery through request submission, broker processing, verification, search-engine refresh, and recurrence. Its original contribution is a stage-based timeline that prevents readers from confusing a dashboard update or first report with confirmed source deletion.

## Evidence-supported conclusion

There is no single reliable completion date. A first scan or status report may arrive within days, and some broker listings disappear quickly, but other removals take weeks or months. A service may also need a later rescan to verify the result. Search engines can continue showing a stale result after the source page changes, and listings can return after an earlier removal. Readers should treat data removal as a maintenance process rather than a one-time countdown.

## Timeline stages

1. **Setup and identity matching:** The user supplies names, addresses, emails, phone numbers, aliases, or authorization details. Missing or delayed verification can slow the process.
2. **Discovery or broad request submission:** Some providers scan public listings first; others submit requests across a supported broker list even when they cannot display the underlying private database.
3. **Broker processing:** Timing depends on each broker. Official provider documentation describes outcomes ranging from immediate removal to weeks or months.
4. **Verification:** A submitted request is not a confirmed removal. Providers may need to rescan a public page or wait for a broker response.
5. **Search refresh:** A source page can be gone while an outdated search result remains until the search engine recrawls it.
6. **Maintenance:** Brokers can rebuild or republish records, making later scans and repeat requests necessary.

## Provider examples

### Incogni

- Incogni says some brokers respond the same day while others take longer, and its dashboard exposes an average resolution time by broker.
- Incogni says delayed or noncompliant broker responses can require follow-up.
- Its current Custom Removals documentation says most eligible removals take 7–30 days and some may take up to 60 days. This custom-removal range must not be generalized to every automated broker request.
- Incogni sends monthly progress emails while a subscription is active.

### Optery

- Optery says paying customers receive full exposure scans and opt-out submissions at least every 30 days, often more frequently during the first month.
- Optery says initial activity is often concentrated in the first one or two weeks, followed by a waiting and verification period.
- Optery says some brokers remove immediately while others take weeks or months.
- Optery's claim that more than half of exposed profiles are typically removed or not found within 30 days is a vendor-reported customer result, not an independent benchmark.
- Extended and Ultimate Removals Reports are generated approximately every three months after the plan has been active long enough; that report cadence is not the same as the removal-submission cadence.

### DeleteMe

- DeleteMe says the first Privacy Report generally arrives within about seven days of signup. It explicitly describes that report as a status update rather than proof that every listing is gone.
- DeleteMe says some brokers act immediately while others take weeks.
- Another current DeleteMe help page says information begins disappearing after the first opt-out round within about six weeks, sometimes faster. This is vendor guidance, not a guarantee for every broker or user.
- DeleteMe says stale search results can take an additional three to four weeks to update after the source site removes the information. This is vendor guidance and should be presented cautiously.

## Independent evidence

Consumer Reports tested seven services and manual opt-outs on 13 people-search sites in 2024. Outcomes changed across one week, one month, and four months, and varied substantially by service. Manual opt-outs removed about 70% of the observed profiles, while paid services ranged from 6% to 68% after four months. The study used a limited sample and older product versions, so it demonstrates variable and incomplete timing rather than a current permanent ranking.

## Government timing context

California's DELETE Act framework requires registered brokers to retrieve requests through DROP beginning August 1, 2026 and process deletion requests within 45 days. This is a legal-processing rule for that system, not a promise that every public page, search result, or unsupported source will disappear within 45 days.

## Claims to avoid

- “Your information will be removed in X days.”
- “The first report proves removal.”
- “A dashboard marked removed guarantees the original record is permanently gone.”
- “All brokers follow legal deadlines.”
- “Search-result disappearance is the same as source deletion.”
- “One successful removal is permanent.”
- Any hands-on or speed claim attributed to Choose Better Tech.
