# Executive Summary

## Overview

Incogni is a beginner-oriented data-removal service focused on automated broker opt-out and deletion requests. The approved research supports positioning it as a credible, automation-first, mainstream privacy tool with stronger-than-average process assurance, not as a service that deletes a person from the internet.

### Evidence

| Classification | Confidence | Last Verified | Summary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-05 | Incogni is a real operating data-removal service with public company materials, a help center, a broker list, and partner distribution through Surfshark / Nord-linked channels. |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-05 | Deloitte's 2025 limited-assurance report verified core operating claims around broker coverage, recurring requests, confirmations, request volume, and non-sale of customer data during 2024. |
| Independent Finding | Moderate | 2026-07-05 | Consumer Reports category research found data-removal services can be incomplete and that manual opt-out can outperform paid services in narrow people-search tests. Incogni was not tested in that experiment. |
| Open Question | Moderate | 2026-07-05 | Incogni-controlled materials conflict on the exact people-search versus public/private broker coverage split. |

## Primary Value Proposition

Incogni's research-backed value proposition is hands-off, recurring data-broker removal for mainstream privacy buyers who want automation, family coverage, public and private broker coverage, dashboard tracking, and some independent process assurance.

## Editorial Summary

The approved research supports framing Incogni as strong for beginners, mainstream privacy buyers, households up to five members, and users who want recurring broker-removal maintenance. It is weaker for people expecting full internet deletion, crisis-level stalking or doxxing help, maximum screenshot-level proof, DIY control, or removal of Google results, public records, court records, government records, social posts, blogs, forums, or dark-web data.

# Strengths

- Strong for beginner-friendly hands-off broker removal.
- Strong for mainstream privacy buyers who want recurring removal requests.
- Strong for users who care about both public and private broker categories.
- Stronger-than-average process assurance through Deloitte limited assurance.
- Useful family plan for up to five total members.
- Good dashboard clarity for mainstream users.
- More international than several U.S.-only competitors in the approved research.

# Limitations

- Does not delete users from the internet.
- Does not guarantee permanent removal.
- Data can reappear.
- Does not directly remove Google search results.
- Does not remove court records, government records, public records, social media posts, blogs, forums, or dark-web data.
- Does not provide maximum screenshot-level proof for every public listing.
- Exact people-search coverage count is inconsistent across Incogni-controlled materials.
- Requires users to trust Incogni with sensitive identifying data.
- Public SOC 2 report evidence was not found in the approved research.

# Ideal Users

- Beginners who want a low-maintenance service.
- Mainstream privacy buyers.
- Users who want both people-search and private-broker removals.
- Households that want up to five people on one plan.
- International users who want some non-U.S. broker-removal coverage.
- Buyers who value third-party process assurance.
- Users who understand data removal is ongoing maintenance.

# Not Recommended For

- Users expecting full internet deletion.
- Crisis-level stalking, doxxing, or executive-protection situations.
- Users whose main issue is Google results, court records, government records, public records, social posts, blogs, forums, or dark-web leaks.
- Users who want maximum proof for every listing.
- DIY users who want full control.
- Users uncomfortable giving a privacy vendor concentrated identifying data.

# Comparative Notes

## DeleteMe

DeleteMe may appeal more to buyers wanting a long-established U.S.-focused service, human-led positioning, flexible multi-person options, and a willingness to pay more. Incogni is usually cleaner for price-conscious, beginner, and international buyers in the approved research.

## Optery

Optery is stronger when the buyer wants screenshot-based proof, exposure discovery, and public-site transparency. Incogni is simpler and lower-friction for mainstream users.

## Privacy Bee

Privacy Bee appears stronger for power users seeking broader feature depth and higher-touch privacy extras. Incogni is cleaner for mainstream affordability and simplicity.

## Kanary

Kanary is stronger for doxxing, harassment, creator, activist, or executive-adjacent threat models. Incogni is better framed as mainstream broker-removal automation.

## Manual Opt-Out

Manual opt-out can be better for exacting control over a limited set of public people-search listings, but it is time-intensive and brittle. Incogni is better for convenience, private-broker coverage, and recurring maintenance.

# Common Misconceptions

- "Incogni deletes me from the internet." It does not.
- "Completed means every copy is gone." The approved research does not support that.
- "Data removal is permanent." Data can reappear.
- "Incogni removes Google results directly." Google search results are separate from source-page removal.
- "Deloitte assurance proves every user outcome." Deloitte supports process claims, not universal outcome guarantees.
- "Protect prevents identity theft." Protect adds identity-related extras, but broker removal is not absolute identity-theft prevention.
