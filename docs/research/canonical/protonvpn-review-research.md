# Executive Summary

## Overview

Proton VPN appears in the approved research as a transparent, privacy-focused VPN with open-source clients, repeated no-logs audits, Swiss jurisdiction, a permanent free tier, and strong privacy positioning. The same research identifies meaningful tradeoffs around price, prorated refunds, support availability, jurisdiction nuance, variable performance, and volatile plan/server details.

### Evidence

| Classification | Confidence | Last Verified | Summary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-02 | Proton VPN is operated by Proton AG, a Swiss technology company that also operates Proton Mail. |
| Verified Fact | High | 2026-07-02 | Proton VPN launched in 2017 as an extension of Proton Mail's privacy-focused services. |
| Independent Finding | High | 2026-07-02 | The research repeatedly characterizes Proton VPN as one of the more transparent privacy-focused VPN providers because of open-source apps, audits, and transparency reporting. |
| Open Question | Moderate | 2026-07-02 | Exact server counts, plan details, device limits, support availability, pricing, and streaming performance are volatile or vary across sources. |

## Primary Value Proposition

Proton VPN's research-backed value proposition is privacy transparency: audited no-logs claims, open-source clients, Swiss jurisdiction, a non-profit-aligned ownership structure, a credible free plan, and strong security fundamentals.

## Editorial Summary

The approved research supports treating Proton VPN as a strong fit for privacy-conscious mainstream users and beginners who value transparency. It should not be framed as perfect, universally fastest, fully anonymous, or always the best value. Pricing, plan structure, refunds, device limits, server counts, countries, and streaming claims must be verified before publication.

# Strengths

- **Verified Fact / Independent Finding:** Strong privacy posture through Securitum no-logs audits, Swiss jurisdiction, open-source apps, and minimal data retention.
- **Independent Finding:** Solid security stack with AES-256/ChaCha20, WireGuard/OpenVPN/IKEv2, kill switch, leak protection, and Secure Core.
- **Independent Finding:** Generous free plan with unlimited data and privacy basics, despite clear limits.
- **Independent Finding:** Good real-world performance for everyday use, though not always top-ranked.
- **Independent Finding:** Clean, intuitive apps suitable for many non-technical users.
- **Independent Finding:** Transparency through open-source clients, audits, and public reporting.

---

# Limitations

- **Independent Finding:** Pricing can be higher than budget VPNs, especially short-term plans.
- **Independent Finding:** Refunds are reported as prorated, which is less generous than flat refund policies.
- **Independent Finding:** Support may not be 24/7 and may be limited to existing subscribers.
- **Independent Finding:** Performance varies, especially latency on long-distance connections.
- **Open Question:** Mixed Trustpilot and app-store ratings suggest unresolved customer-experience concerns.
- **Open Question:** Some servers are rented from third-party data-center providers.
- **Open Question:** Swiss court orders may allow targeted future logging for specific accounts.

---

# Ideal Users

- Privacy-conscious mainstream users who value independent audits, open-source clients, and transparency reporting.
- Users willing to pay a premium for transparency rather than lowest possible price.
- Beginners or low-budget users who need a reputable permanent free plan and can accept limits.
- Travelers and remote workers using untrusted Wi-Fi who need encryption, leak protection, and mainstream usability.

---

# Not Recommended For

- Price-sensitive users focused mainly on cheapest streaming access.
- Users who require 24/7 live support.
- Highly latency-sensitive competitive gamers.
- Users in highly restrictive regimes who would depend on one VPN alone.
