## Independent protection evidence

- AV-TEST April 2026: TotalAV scored 6/6 protection, 6/6 performance, and 6/6 usability on Windows.
- AV-Comparatives July-October 2025: 99.5% real-world protection in a 428-case window.
- AV-Comparatives 2025 annual summary: two Advanced+ awards and one Advanced award; Approved Security Product, not Top-Rated.
- SE Labs December 2025: 97% protection accuracy, 100% legitimate accuracy, 99% total accuracy, AAA award; it missed one targeted attack.
- Virus Bulletin February 2025: version 6.2.121 passed VB100 with A+ malware-detection grade. Current category participation should be rechecked rather than implied from an older result.
- These results establish a credible current product, not guaranteed protection, best-in-class continuity, or CBT-tested performance.

## Product and platform scope

- Official help documents Windows, macOS, Android, iOS/iPadOS, and Fire HD availability.
- Real-time antivirus is documented for Windows, macOS, and Android.
- TotalAV explicitly says its iOS app does not run an antivirus; its iOS tools have a different scope.
- Current requirements say ARM-based Windows devices are not supported.
- WebShield, scanning, quarantine, cloud scanning, breach monitoring, VPN, ad blocking, and other features are documented, but plan and platform availability differ.
- CBT did not install or inspect any app and does not make ease, speed, resource-use, alert-quality, or reliability claims.

## Subscription, renewal, cancellation, and refunds

- Official terms were last updated February 25, 2026.
- Subscriptions generally auto-renew; promotional first terms renew at the then-current standard price shown or communicated.
- Annual and multi-year renewal notices may be sent up to 30 days before renewal; monthly plans renew without an additional monthly notice under the general terms.
- Multiple services and add-ons are treated as separate subscriptions and may require separate cancellation.
- Annual or biannual purchases generally have a 30-day refund-request window; monthly, quarterly, and add-on services generally have a 14-day window, subject to eligibility and purchase channel.
- Turning off auto-renewal does not itself request a refund. App-store purchases follow the store's process.
- No volatile numeric checkout price is suitable for a durable verdict without a dated, region-specific checkout capture.

## Privacy and trust

- The privacy policy documents account/contact/payment information and automatic data such as IP address, browser, device, pages/activity, cookies, and potentially location from mobile devices.
- It says personal information is shared for cross-context behavioral or targeted advertising as described, while offering applicable opt-out rights.
- A privacy policy describes commitments and collection categories; it is not independent proof of perfect practice.

## Competitor context

- Microsoft Defender is the lower-complexity baseline for many Windows users.
- Bitdefender has stronger 2025 annual AV-Comparatives standing in the current category research.
- Norton is a stronger full-suite recommendation when its bundle earns renewal cost.
- ESET, F-Secure, and McAfee remain credible fit alternatives.
- The review may link to Best Antivirus Software but must not rerank the market.

## Best-fit users

- Readers who specifically want TotalAV, understand its plan and add-on structure, verify renewal, and accept the documented privacy/platform tradeoffs.
- Buyers who value its current protection evidence more than annual award leadership.

## Poor-fit users

- Windows users satisfied with Defender.
- Buyers who want the strongest annual multi-lab standing in CBT's current ranking.
- Readers who dislike separate subscriptions or promotional-to-renewal changes.
- ARM-based Windows users.
- Anyone expecting an iOS app to perform Windows-style antivirus scanning.

## Provisional verdict

TotalAV is a legitimate antivirus with strong recent lab evidence. It is not CBT's leading recommendation because its 2025 annual standing trails the top group, its SE Labs result was strong rather than perfect, and its renewal/add-on structure requires more buyer attention. It can be a defensible purchase for a well-informed user who specifically prefers it, but Defender, Bitdefender, or Norton will be the clearer fit for many readers.
