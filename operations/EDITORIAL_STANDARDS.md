# Choose Better Tech Editorial Standards

Version: 1.0
Status: Binding
Owner: Choose Better Tech
Effective Date: 2026-07-12

## Mission

Choose Better Tech is an independent software review, comparison, and buyer-education publication for beginner and mainstream software buyers.

Its purpose is to help readers make informed decisions through evidence, clear tradeoffs, practical guidance, and transparent limitations.

## Reader-First Principle

Recommendations must optimize for reader fit, not publisher revenue.

Every article should leave the reader with more understanding than they had before reading it. Content must teach, not pressure.

## Editorial Independence

Affiliate availability, commission rate, advertiser interest, network approval, conversion potential, or commercial relationship must never influence:

- topic selection;
- candidate inclusion;
- candidate exclusion;
- ranking position;
- recommendation label;
- criticism;
- verdict;
- conclusion;
- evidence selection;
- CTA placement;
- update priority.

Choose Better Tech does not accept payment for positive reviews, guarantee positive coverage, or allow advertisers to approve editorial conclusions. Affiliate and editorial processes must remain separate.

## Accuracy

Do not invent:

- facts;
- features;
- prices;
- plan limits;
- ownership;
- audits;
- incidents;
- testing;
- benchmarks;
- availability;
- legal conclusions;
- indexing status;
- traffic;
- conversions;
- revenue.

When evidence is uncertain, preserve uncertainty. When sources conflict, document the conflict. When a claim cannot be verified, omit it or label it clearly.

## Testing Transparency

Never imply hands-on testing unless it actually occurred. Clearly distinguish:

- documentation review;
- independent-source review;
- live product testing;
- account-level feature testing;
- benchmark testing;
- security testing;
- visual or site testing.

If no hands-on product testing occurred, say so explicitly.

## Fit-Based Recommendations

There is rarely one universally best product. Recommendations must identify:

- who benefits;
- who should skip it;
- important limitations;
- meaningful alternatives;
- material tradeoffs.

Do not force a winner where the evidence supports a conditional conclusion.

## Security and Privacy Language

Do not use unsupported absolute language such as:

- `unhackable`;
- `completely anonymous`;
- `totally secure`;
- `guaranteed private`;
- `military-grade` as proof of quality;
- `zero logs` without qualification;
- `zero knowledge` as a universal certification;
- `streaming guaranteed`;
- `parental control replacement`;
- `backup replacement`.

Distinguish:

- security from privacy;
- encryption at rest from end-to-end encryption;
- authentication from encryption;
- audits from guarantees;
- open source from verified operational behavior;
- sync from backup;
- device limits from account-sharing rights;
- recovery from emergency access;
- introductory price from renewal price.

## Breaches and Incidents

Do not treat all breaches as equivalent. Distinguish:

- website breach;
- account-database breach;
- employee compromise;
- source-code theft;
- development-environment compromise;
- encrypted-vault theft;
- plaintext exposure;
- metadata exposure;
- session-token theft;
- supply-chain compromise;
- credential stuffing;
- phishing.

When discussing an incident, explain what was accessed, what was encrypted, what remained exposed, what users needed to do, and what remains uncertain.

## Commercial Tone

Avoid:

- fake urgency;
- countdown framing;
- unsupported savings claims;
- manipulative fear;
- excessive purchase buttons;
- affiliate CTAs inside neutral evidence, security, privacy, breach, recovery, child-safety, or methodology sections.

## Corrections

When an error is found:

- correct it promptly;
- record the change;
- update the checked date;
- preserve significant correction history when appropriate;
- do not silently rewrite major conclusions without documentation.

## Publication Labels

Only use approved status labels defined in `operations/PUBLISHING_WORKFLOW.md`.

A deployed page is not automatically editorially approved.
