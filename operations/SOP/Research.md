# SOP: Research

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active  
Owner: Choose Better Tech  
Last Updated: 2026-07-02

## Purpose

Define the standard research workflow for Choose Better Tech articles.

## Scope

This SOP applies to product reviews, buyer guides, comparisons, and explanatory articles.

## Procedure

| Step | Action | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Define the reader decision question. | Research objective. |
| 2 | Run Perplexity Deep Research for the topic. | Initial research notes. |
| 3 | Review product documentation, pricing pages, and support materials. | Verified facts. |
| 4 | Review independent reviews and credible third-party sources. | External context. |
| 5 | Review community feedback where relevant. | User experience signals. |
| 6 | Identify evidence gaps and conflicting claims. | Research risk notes. |
| 7 | Create final research deliverables. | Research file, source log, and summary. |

## Perplexity Deep Research

- Start with the article's decision question.
- Ask for product capabilities, pricing considerations, limitations, and alternatives.
- Request source-backed claims.
- Treat the output as a research starting point, not final evidence.

## Research Review

- Confirm claims against primary sources where possible.
- Separate facts from interpretation.
- Flag outdated or unsupported information.
- Record open questions before briefing.

## Evidence Validation

- Prefer official documentation for product features and pricing.
- Use independent sources for reputation, security, usability, and market context.
- Avoid relying on affiliate roundups as primary evidence.
- Document source dates when freshness matters.

## Deliverables

- [ ] Research objective.
- [ ] Research summary.
- [ ] Source log.
- [ ] Evidence gaps.
- [ ] Recommended article angle.
- [ ] Questions for editorial review.
