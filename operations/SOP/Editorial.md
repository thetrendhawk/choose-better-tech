# SOP: Editorial

Version: 1.0  
Status: Active  
Owner: Choose Better Tech  
Last Updated: 2026-07-02

## Purpose

Define the editorial workflow for turning research into publishable articles.

## Procedure

| Step | Action | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Review the research file and source log. | Research understanding. |
| 2 | Define the target reader and decision question. | Editorial angle. |
| 3 | Create the editorial brief. | Approved brief. |
| 4 | Synthesize evidence into recommendations and tradeoffs. | Article structure. |
| 5 | Apply editorial standards. | Draft-ready outline. |
| 6 | Confirm disclosure and trust requirements. | Publication-ready brief. |

## Brief Creation

- Use the brief template.
- State the reader problem clearly.
- Define who the article is for and who should avoid the recommendation.
- Include required sections, internal links, and SEO notes.

## Research Synthesis

- Convert research into decision guidance.
- Explain tradeoffs plainly.
- Identify confidence level.
- Note where evidence is limited.

## Editorial Standards

- Write for beginners.
- Avoid hype, fear tactics, and unsupported superlatives.
- Make recommendations conditional and contextual.
- Include visible affiliate disclosure where relevant.
- Ensure the article remains useful even without affiliate links.
