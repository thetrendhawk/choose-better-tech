# Choose Better Tech Monetization Research Standard

Version: 1.0
Status: Binding
Owner: Choose Better Tech
Effective Date: 2026-07-12

## Purpose

Monetization research is a required parallel workstream for every substantial content cluster and for substantial commercial article research, including rankings, comparisons, reviews, and buying guides with meaningful product-selection intent.

Its purpose is to identify ethical business opportunities without allowing commercial availability or value to shape editorial conclusions.

Monetization research must remain structurally separate from editorial research, claim ledgers, candidate decisions, scoring, verdicts, and publication conclusions.

## Permitted Business Influence

Verified monetization evidence may influence:

- business planning;
- application priorities;
- category-economics analysis;
- maintenance investment;
- commercial outreach priorities;
- revenue-model experiments;
- sponsorship inventory planning;
- owned-product planning;
- commercial operations capacity.

It may not influence:

- factual conclusions;
- candidate inclusion;
- candidate exclusion;
- rankings;
- verdicts;
- recommendation labels;
- evidence interpretation;
- publication conclusions.

Affiliate availability, commission rate, advertiser interest, approval status, sponsorship potential, lead value, or conversion potential also may not influence topic selection, criticism, CTA prominence, source selection, or update priority.

## Required Parallel Research Scope

Substantial cluster and commercial-article workflows must investigate, where relevant:

- provider-level affiliate programs;
- provider-level referral programs;
- direct programs;
- Impact;
- CJ Affiliate;
- Awin;
- PartnerStack;
- ShareASale or its current successor state;
- FlexOffers;
- Rakuten Advertising;
- Tradedoubler;
- Webgains;
- Sovrn Commerce;
- Skimlinks;
- other relevant affiliate networks;
- software marketplaces;
- reseller or channel programs;
- direct sponsorships;
- display advertising;
- consent-based lead generation;
- newsletter sponsorships and placements;
- premium guides;
- memberships;
- tools and calculators;
- templates and checklists;
- reports and datasets;
- licensing and syndication;
- consulting, setup, or other services;
- other realistic and ethical revenue models relevant to the category.

Research must extend beyond CBT's current partnerships and network approvals. Existing commercial relationships cannot define the provider universe.

## Default Records

A substantial cluster must maintain a monetization opportunity map at:

`docs/monetization/<cluster>-monetization-opportunity-map.md`

An article may use the approved cluster map when it is current and sufficiently specific. If the article introduces a new provider, audience, offer type, or commercial model, add an article-specific record or update the cluster map before commercial implementation.

The editorial research record may link to the monetization record but must not copy commission or approval information into candidate-selection or verdict reasoning.

## Provider-Level Required Fields

For every provider or material opportunity reviewed, record:

- provider or opportunity;
- monetization model;
- direct program or network;
- network or platform;
- public program availability;
- application requirements;
- commission or revenue model;
- recurring or one-time treatment;
- cookie duration;
- geographic restrictions;
- traffic or audience requirements;
- payout terms;
- promotional restrictions;
- paid-search or brand-bidding rules;
- CBT current status;
- evidence source;
- checked date;
- conflict or compliance risk;
- recommended action.

Allowed recommended actions are:

- `Already active`;
- `Apply now`;
- `Apply after traffic growth`;
- `Monitor`;
- `Direct outreach`;
- `Test later`;
- `Not a fit`;
- `Unavailable`;
- `Status unverified`.

## Public Availability Versus CBT Status

Keep these facts separate:

1. whether a provider or network publicly offers a program;
2. whether CBT has an account with the network;
3. whether CBT applied to the provider;
4. whether CBT was approved;
5. whether a specific product or plan is commissionable;
6. whether a tracked destination is verified and authorized for implementation.

A public application page does not prove CBT eligibility, acceptance, product coverage, payout eligibility, or permission to use a tracked link.

Repository records and explicit account evidence are the source of truth for CBT status. Do not infer account state from a public program page.

## Evidence and Conflict Rules

Prefer current official provider terms, official network listings, official publisher documentation, and authenticated program terms.

Do not invent or infer:

- commission rates;
- recurring eligibility;
- cookie duration;
- attribution rules;
- application approval;
- program availability;
- payout timing or thresholds;
- geographic eligibility;
- traffic requirements;
- paid-search permission;
- brand-bidding permission;
- CBT acceptance or active status.

When sources conflict:

- record both sources;
- identify their dates and authority where available;
- state which source is newer or more authoritative;
- preserve remaining uncertainty;
- use `Status unverified` when the conflict prevents a safe conclusion;
- never select the commercially favorable version by default.

Search snippets, unauthenticated directories, old promotional pages, and community reports may identify a lead but do not authorize implementation.

## Non-Affiliate Revenue Assessment

Every substantial cluster map must evaluate realistic non-affiliate models, including:

- display ads;
- sponsorships;
- newsletters;
- lead generation;
- premium guides;
- memberships;
- calculators or tools;
- templates and checklists;
- reports and datasets;
- licensing and syndication;
- consulting or setup services;
- other category-specific models.

For each model, record:

- revenue model;
- audience or traffic prerequisite;
- operational burden;
- reader-value case;
- privacy, disclosure, editorial, and compliance risks;
- required controls;
- recommended action.

Unrealistic, high-liability, low-trust, or operationally unsuitable models must be labeled `Not a fit` rather than included to make the opportunity set look larger.

## Sponsorship, Lead, and Service Safeguards

- Sponsorship buys labeled inventory, not coverage, inclusion, rankings, wording, review access, or favorable conclusions.
- Vendor-funded research requires disclosure, CBT control of methodology, and the right to publish unfavorable results or decline publication.
- Newsletter addresses may not be sold or transferred without explicit informed consent.
- Lead generation requires affirmative consent that names the recipient and states what data will be shared.
- A guide download, calculator use, or ordinary newsletter signup is not consent to sell a lead.
- Consulting or setup services may not require CBT to receive passwords, recovery codes, encryption keys, or private file contents.
- Reseller and managed-service models require a separate conflict, support, liability, and brand-separation review.

## Monetization Independence Gate

Every substantial cluster opportunity map must contain exactly one current gate near the beginning:

`Monetization Independence Check: PASS`

or:

`Monetization Independence Check: FAIL`

The check is `PASS` only when:

- the editorial candidate set was not built from monetization availability;
- non-affiliate candidates received equal consideration;
- commission or commercial value did not affect evidence interpretation;
- public program availability is separate from CBT status;
- conflicts and unknowns remain visible;
- no commercial implementation was performed under research authority;
- the author and reviewer found no hidden commercial influence on editorial output.

A `FAIL` blocks affiliate implementation, sponsorship, lead generation, commercial CTAs, and other monetization deployment until the cause is corrected and independently reviewed.

## Research Does Not Authorize Implementation

Completing monetization research does not authorize:

- program applications;
- vendor outreach;
- negotiation;
- tracked-link insertion;
- affiliate-registry changes;
- CTA changes;
- sponsorship acceptance;
- lead collection or transfer;
- ranking changes;
- verdict changes;
- recommendation-label changes;
- publication changes.

Each action requires a separate explicit authorization, current terms review, compliance check, and appropriate repository workflow.

## Independent Review

A separate reviewer must challenge:

- unsupported public-program claims;
- stale or conflicting network listings;
- invented or ambiguous commission, cookie, attribution, or payout terms;
- assumptions about CBT eligibility or approval;
- hidden editorial influence;
- unrealistic non-affiliate opportunities;
- disclosure, privacy, brand-bidding, and regulatory risks;
- separation of public program availability from CBT status;
- the Monetization Independence Check.

The author may not self-approve. Allowed review decisions are:

- `RETURN FOR CORRECTION`;
- `APPROVE FOR MERGE`.

All required corrections must be completed before approval.

## Application and Implementation Gate

Before a later authorized commercial action:

1. recheck the official program and authenticated terms;
2. confirm CBT's actual network and advertiser status;
3. capture commission, attribution, cookie, validation, payout, geographic, traffic, promotional, and paid-search terms;
4. complete disclosure and privacy review;
5. verify the editorial output was finalized independently;
6. add or change tracked destinations only through the centralized affiliate registry;
7. run the affiliate-independence and raw-link scans;
8. document the authorized action and result in operations records.

## Refresh Triggers

Refresh monetization research:

- before every application or outreach action;
- before adding or changing a tracked destination;
- before a commercial article or cluster launch when the map is older than one quarter;
- when a provider changes ownership, plans, network, commissions, cookie duration, attribution, payout, geography, or promotional terms;
- when a network closes, migrates, or changes publisher requirements;
- when CBT reaches a relevant traffic, newsletter, sponsorship, or display-ad threshold;
- at least quarterly while the cluster is actively expanding.

## Failure Handling

Use `BLOCKED` when:

- required monetization research is missing;
- the independence check is `FAIL`;
- public program facts and CBT status are conflated;
- a required independent review is missing;
- unresolved evidence would make implementation unsafe;
- implementation is attempted without separate authorization.

Research may remain complete while a program is `Status unverified`; uncertainty blocks only the unsupported commercial action, not independent editorial work.
